Introduction

Well-adapted students consistently maintain academic behaviors to achieve desirable grades. First-year students, however, are among the majority still discovering suitable studying habits to strike a balance between academics and their personal lives. The unfamiliar environment and schooling pose serious threats to a first-year student’s successful transition to university. Consequently, many high-achieving students wish to safeguard their academic futures by adjusting as seamlessly as possible. Considering these ongoing debates, we aim to initiate our discussion by examining the factors that contribute to varying levels of success among students during their initial year at McGill from a statistical perspective. To objectively measure success, we have chosen to focus on first-year student GPA as our dependent variable. This statistical dilemma left us with the following question:

What factors contribute to a first-year McGill student’s GPA?

Our Independent Variables

For our analysis, we have collected data from McGill students in second year or higher to determine relationships between first year GPA and a number of variables. By using a convenience sampling design, we created an anonymous survey that collected the following independent variables:

Gender
Highschool Average
Hours of Studying per Week
Hours of Sleep per Day
Daily Cups of Coffee
Missed Lectures per Week


Through conducting a regression analysis, we aim to obtain answers to our research question and derive insights that will enable first-year students to improve their GPA. Taking these facts into account, we aspire for our research to aid students in enhancing their GPA by fostering the cultivation of beneficial habits that will serve them well throughout their university journey.
